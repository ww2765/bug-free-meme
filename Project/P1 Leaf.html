This dried leaf was found near the edge of the garden bed. Its surface is brittle and papery, the veins pronounced from drying — a small record of the season's late heat and wind.

Why I Collected It

  • Feels fragile yet comforting to hold
  • Vein patterning like a map
  • Represents seasonal change and the passage of time

Fun fact: A dried leaf still “remembers” the tree it came from—its vein pattern acts like a fingerprint, unique to the species (and sometimes even the individual tree).”
